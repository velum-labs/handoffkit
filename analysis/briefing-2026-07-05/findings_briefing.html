3…but public data cannot pick the panel (settled, twice)

Checkpoints C2 + C2V: choose a panel on half the tasks, score it on the held-out half, against the dumb baseline "take the top-K models by average score." 6Measurement integrity: the truncation ladder

Our single most repeated lesson: thinking-style models produce garbage numbers unless completion budgets are huge — and published benchmark scores rarely disclose this. We enforce: >10% truncated rows ⇒ pass rate refused.

7The wildcard: synthesis once beat the theoretical selection ceiling

In the original C3 replay, the fused answer stream scored above the frozen bank's oracle — impossible for any pick-the-best-candidate scheme, possible only if synthesis combines partial answers into new passing ones.

Best single member
28.1%
Oracle ceiling (selection max)
35.1%
Fused output (judged replay)
38.6%
capture 150%
Why this is "directional", not proven: the replay used a GPT-5.5 fallback judge (the committed Kimi judge failed on a malformed response), and some uplift came from the judge synthesizing answers itself rather than pure selection. The pre-registered, leak-free protocol to test this cleanly is exactly what capture-pilot-1 (paused, 29/60 member rows done, $0.51 spent) is designed to run. If it replicates, the product story upgrades from "cheaper" to "better and cheaper" — the single highest-leverage open question in the program.

Each path states what it buys, what it costs, and what would kill it. They are mostly compatible; the real discussion is sequencing and where the launch claim comes from.

10Discussion questions for this meeting

  1. Launch shape: is v1 "predefined fused ensembles" (wait for C+E) or "honest routing + DIY ensembles now" (path D) with fusion as v1.1? What is the minimum evidence bar we're comfortable putting our name on?
  2. The capture bet: if pilot-1 shows capture ≥100% replicates, synthesis design becomes our moat — do we then invest in judge/synthesis R&D as a first-class workstream (prompts, multi-round synthesis, non-member judges)?
  3. Domain priority: repo bugfix has the best headroom+demand but needs harness work; terminal/agentic is second and partially covered by public infrastructure. Do we accept the engineering cost of B before any launch-grade number exists?
  4. Price anchor: which frontier model do we price against on the card — claude-opus-4.1 (the scan's anchor), gpt-5.5, or "cheapest frontier that beats our score"? This choice defines the $/solve headline.
  5. Kimi asset: "kimi-k2-thinking is unmeasurable single-shot below 64k" is a finding nobody else publishes. Do we use measurement-integrity results as public content (credibility play), or keep them internal?
  6. Spend authority: the next ~$65 (A + C) is decision-grade; Step 5 is $100–500. Approve the full funnel now, or gate at C's result?
